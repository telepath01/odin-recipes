Pastelon (Puertorican Lasagna)

The Pastelon is a Christmas special within the Puerto Rican community. This delicacy is made from ground beef and uses plantains as the layers for the lasagna. The meat is seasoned to perfection by adding various spices and vegetables. The meat is then cooked slowly till complete. The dish is then ready to prepare. You start by cutting your plantains in strips, then you fry the plantains and lay them in the baking pan. After the first layer is placed then you place a layer of the meat. This process is repeated till the top layer is plantains. Lastly you take three eggs and whip them, pour the eggs on the dish and cook for 30 minutes or until golden brown at 350 degrees.

    Ingrediants

  • Ground Beef
  • Green Bell Peper
  • Garlic
  • Adobo
  • Rasins
  • Ripe Plantains
  • Eggs

    Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. In a bowl mix the beef with the bell peppers, garlic and adobo.
  3. Cook beef until browned. Stir in raisins.
  4. Cut plantains into slices, use oil to cook plantains in a pan untill golden brown on each side.
  5. Assemble the dish by placing a layer of plantains followed by the beef. Repeat till you finish with the plantains on top.
  6. Take 3 eggs and beat until evenly mixed. Pour the beaten eggs over the dish.
  7. Place baking pan in oven for 30 minutes.
  8. Upon completion cut into portions and enjoy.
